Aerobic Fitness
Aerobic excercise causes you to breath faster and more deeply. The better your aerobic fitness, the more efficiently your heart, lungs and blood vessels transport oxygen throughout your body and the easier it is to complete routine physical tasks.
Aerobic activity includes any physical activity that uses large muscle groups and increases heart rate.
  • Walking
  • Jogging
  • Swimming
  • Dancing
Are all good examples of aerobic excercises.
Strength Training
Strength training is another key component of a fitness training program. It can help you increase bone strength and muscular fitness, as well as helping you to manage or lose weight.
  • Resistance Bands
  • Free Weights
  • Weight Machines
Are all good examples of tools to use for strength training.
You could also try:
  • Pushups
  • Pullups
  • Leg Squats
  • Crunches/Situps
Body weight workouts are a great way to work on strength training!
Core Excercises
The muscles in your abdomen, lower back and pelvis (Your Core Muscles) help protect your back and connect upper and lower body movements. Core strength is a key element of a well-rounded fitness training program.
A core exercise is any exercise that uses the trunk of your body without support.
  • Bridges
  • Planks
  • Situps
  • Fitness Ball Exercises
Core workouts allow you to use your upper and lower body muscles more effectively.
Balance Training
Balance exercises can help you maintain your balance at any age. Generally a good idea for older adults in particular to include exercises to maintain or improve balance.
  • Standing on One Leg
  • Tai Chi
Are some examples of exercises that can help improve your balance.
Flexibility and Stretching
Flexibility is an important aspect of physical fitness, and it's a good idea to include stretching and flexibility activities in a fitness program. Stretching and flexibility can make it easier for you to do many everyday activities as well as increase range of motions.
Consider stretching after you exercise as your muscles will be warmed up and more receptive to it. However, if you wish to stretch before a workout, do a quick warm up, like walking, 5 to 10 minutes prior to your workout.
Yoga is a great activity to increase flexibility.
